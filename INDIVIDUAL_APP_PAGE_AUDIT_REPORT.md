# JRP Apps Individual Page Content and SEO Audit

**Baseline:** `jrphub-apps-audited-2026-07-18.zip`  
**Audit date:** July 18, 2026  
**Scope:** The eight individual app and game pages under `/apps/`  
**Preservation rule:** Existing design, colors, layout, spacing, animations, navigation, URLs, folder structure and responsive breakpoints were retained.

## Executive Summary

All eight individual product pages were expanded from roughly 763–789 words to 1,079–1,200 words of main-page content. Each page now explains the product purpose, the problem it addresses, practical benefits, user experience, detailed feature value, intended audience, privacy approach, offline use, Android performance and update expectations.

Every page now contains eight useful visible FAQs. The FAQPage JSON-LD is generated from the same question-and-answer content, so the visible FAQ text and schema text match exactly. Related products are presented as four structured cards with an icon, title, description, explicit user benefit and descriptive “View [Product]” link.

No product capabilities, exact numeric versions or release dates were invented. Live pages display “Latest Google Play release” in the Version field. Coming-soon pages display “Pre-release development.”

## Improvements by Page

### Arrow Escape

- Expanded the overview around direction-based movement, blocked paths, move order and spatial reasoning.
- Explained why progressive levels, responsive touch feedback, quick sessions and offline-friendly play matter.
- Added audience examples for casual players, students and self-paced puzzle fans.
- Added privacy language for single-player use, unnecessary-permission avoidance and product-specific policy access.
- Added natural links to Sudoku, Water Sort Master and ChessMaster.

### Calculator Pro

- Expanded the overview around standard and scientific work, readable expressions, history and reusable memory values.
- Explained the practical value of each calculation feature rather than using one-line bullets.
- Added audience examples for students, engineers, shop owners, professionals and households.
- Added contextual links to Money Mate and Sudoku.
- Added privacy and offline explanations suitable for calculations involving personal, work or study information.

### ChessMaster

- Expanded the planned product purpose around rule familiarity, board readability, tactics and self-paced practice.
- Clearly retained the coming-soon status and avoided unsupported release or numeric-version claims.
- Added audience guidance for beginners, students, returning players and users preparing for competitive play.
- Added contextual links to Sudoku, Arrow Escape and Water Sort Master.
- Linked privacy guidance to the existing JRP Apps privacy directory until a dedicated ChessMaster policy is published.

### Fit Mate

- Expanded the overview around routine consistency, activity records and clear progress summaries.
- Added audience examples for beginners, regular gym users and people returning to activity.
- Included a clear statement that the app is an organisational companion and not medical advice.
- Added privacy explanations for personal fitness records and unnecessary-permission avoidance.
- Added contextual links to Money Mate, Calculator Pro and Arrow Escape.

### Money Mate

- Expanded the overview around transaction capture, categories, financial patterns and practical personal record keeping.
- Explained why quick entry, history, categories, reports and simple budgeting support matter.
- Added audience examples for individuals, families, students and freelancers.
- Clarified that Money Mate is a personal tracker, not a bank, accounting service or financial-advice provider.
- Added contextual links to Calculator Pro, Fit Mate and Sudoku.

### Style Text Pro

- Expanded the planned app purpose around Unicode transformation, copy-ready text and compatibility limits.
- Explained live previews, decorative variations, symbols and direct copying in practical terms.
- Added realistic audience examples for profiles, captions, gaming names and short creative headings.
- Clarified that Unicode appearance depends on the receiving app, device and font.
- Added contextual links to Calculator Pro, Money Mate and Water Sort Master.

### Sudoku

- Expanded the planned game purpose around classic 9 × 9 rules, logical deduction, notes and difficulty levels.
- Explained why candidate notes, hints, progress and board readability matter.
- Added audience guidance for beginners, experienced solvers, students and self-paced puzzle fans.
- Clarified that Sudoku depends on logic rather than advanced arithmetic.
- Added contextual links to Calculator Pro, Arrow Escape and Water Sort Master.

### Water Sort Master

- Expanded the overview around legal pours, empty-space management and colour sequencing.
- Explained progressive layouts, experimentation, calm interaction and flexible session length.
- Added audience guidance for casual players, puzzle fans and users who prefer low-pressure play.
- Added privacy and offline explanations for self-contained single-player puzzle use.
- Added contextual links to Arrow Escape, Sudoku and ChessMaster.

## Common Improvements Applied to Every Page

1. A more specific, unique SEO title and meta description.
2. A richer overview explaining purpose, problem, benefits and user experience.
3. Six expanded feature explanations with practical value.
4. A detailed “Who Should Use It” section with examples.
5. Privacy-first content covering local use, permissions, data control and policy access.
6. Performance content covering startup, lightweight behaviour, battery considerations, Android optimisation, responsive UI and Material Design.
7. An offline-use and updates section.
8. A lazy-loaded product preview with descriptive alt text and a visible caption.
9. Eight visible FAQs exactly matching FAQPage schema.
10. Four related-product cards with icons, descriptions, benefits and descriptive page links.
11. Visible Version, Last updated, Android requirement and Developer fields.
12. Updated SoftwareApplication data including dateModified, softwareVersion, softwareRequirements and language.
